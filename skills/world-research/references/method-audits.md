## Scientific studies and quantitative papers

Inspect the full relevant paper and any accessible supplement. Record the research question, design, sample, unit, population, period, exposure or intervention, comparator, outcomes, measurement instruments, model or identification strategy, effect sizes, uncertainty, robustness, missingness, confounder handling, preregistration, data/code availability, funding, conflicts, replication, corrections, retractions, and external validity.

Test whether the public claim changes correlation into causation, a subgroup into a population, relative change into absolute magnitude, statistical significance into practical importance, or a modeled estimate into an observed fact.

## Datasets, polls, indices, and models

Inspect the codebook, version, row or unit definition, denominator, collection method, weighting, missingness, revisions, transformations, category rules, model assumptions, validation, uncertainty, and sensitivity to plausible alternatives. Reproduce material totals or classifications when possible.

For polls, inspect question wording, order, population, sampling frame, mode, field dates, weighting, likely-voter screen, nonresponse, design effects, sponsor, and topline versus cross-tab fit.

For ratings and indices, audit indicator selection, coding rules, coder disagreement, imputation, aggregation, weighting, governance, funding, and hard cases that test consistency.

## Government and institutional records

Separate authority, intention, announcement, appropriation, obligation, implementation, enforcement, output, and outcome. A press release can establish an announcement or institutional position; it cannot alone establish operational continuity, adequacy, compliance, or causal effect.

Inspect underlying orders, budgets, contracts, administrative data, dockets, audit reports, implementation logs, inspector-general findings, or local records where the claim depends on performance.

## Legal records

Identify the court, docket, procedural posture, burden and standard of proof, allegations, admitted evidence, findings of fact, legal rule, application, holding, remedy, dicta, dissent, unresolved issues, appellate status, and what the decision did not decide. Do not treat an allegation, filing, judicial observation, verdict, or appellate holding as interchangeable.

## Corporate and financial records

Inspect primary filings, footnotes, accounting definitions, period changes, reconciliations, adjusted versus unadjusted metrics, cash flow, debt terms, covenants, contingencies, auditor language, management incentives, related-party issues, and subsequent filings. Reconcile company explanations with operational and market data.

## Journalism, transcripts, and audiovisual evidence

Trace reported claims to original documents, datasets, named sources, full interviews, recordings, photographs, or contemporaneous records where possible. Inspect quotation context and chronology. Distinguish firsthand reporting, anonymous characterization, analysis, and repetition of another outlet.

For audiovisual evidence, record provenance, edits, timestamp, surrounding context, transcription method, uncertainty, and whether tone or visual inference exceeds what can be established.

## Historical evidence

Separate contemporaneous records, retrospective testimony, archival absence, quantitative reconstruction, and later interpretation. Establish chronology, geography, institutions, material conditions, affected groups, mechanisms, and major scholarly disputes. Test analogies through both similarities and differences; a resemblance does not establish the same cause or likely outcome.

## Hidden-purpose and implementation claims

Sample underlying grants, contracts, programs, facilities, permits, local decisions, budgets, implementers, subimplementers, activities, and outcomes. Compare stated purpose with documented activity. Look for concealment indicators, but do not infer covert purpose merely from opacity, euphemistic language, or strategic alignment.

For implementation-dependent national claims, inspect enough concrete named cases to test whether the general narrative survives local records.
