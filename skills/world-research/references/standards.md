# External Journalism and Verification Standards

Use these as professional process references, not as authorities that determine substantive conclusions.

- Society of Professional Journalists: verify before publishing, prefer original sources, provide context, identify sources, distinguish advocacy, and correct errors.
- International Fact-Checking Network: nonpartisanship, source transparency, funding transparency, methodology transparency, and corrections.
- Verification Handbook and Verification Handbook 2: digital-source verification, provenance, corroboration, and documented methods.
- Reuters Trust Principles: stated institutional commitments to independence, integrity, reliability, and freedom from bias; audit actual reporting claim by claim rather than assuming compliance.

## Educational standard

- A Writer Research Packet transfers knowledge; its audit files provide verification. The reader should not need to read every source or CSV to understand the background, mechanism, evidence, uncertainty, and article relevance.
- Teach before compressing. Tables, verdict ledgers, and source IDs may recap a prose explanation but may not introduce unfamiliar cases or institutions by themselves.
- Validate the packet with an independent reader who sees only the packet. Revise the packet whenever that reader cannot explain what happened, who wanted what, what evidence supports the account, what remains disputed, and why it matters.
